So, this will serve as something of an aid for you as you try to get in better shape and maintain your physical well being. We have discussed at length the reasons to exercise beyond maintaining your weight but to briefly recap some of them, here are some compelling reasons to develop your strength endurance/coordination.

  • maintain control of own body
  • maintain ability move around world with relative ease
  • muscular men cant be called "dadbod"
  • prevent immobility
  • keep metabolism at current or higher levels
  • it feels good
  • your doctor will like you more for it
  • good for confidence
  • good way to spend time, similiar to walking

Okay now that we have covered those reasons briefly, we will get into the how. Theres a lot to learn but you dont need all that to be honest, you just need a simple easy program you can loosely follow, and given adequate effort and a proper diet, you can make a lot of progress. I promise. So, theres more than one way to organize things, but id like to focus for now on the mechanics of individual movements. As we have mentioned before, the reason to incorporate things like squats, pushing exercises, and pulling exercises, is because those are the things your body ought to be able to do. Makes sense. When we perform corresponding exercises, we practice those movement patterns thus training and maintaining our ability to do them. That is to say we hold onto our ability to do the things we ought to be able to do. We wouldnt want to be one of those guys leaned over 90 degrees resting on his shopping cart, would we?
